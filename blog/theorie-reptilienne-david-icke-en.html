David Icke's Reptilian Theory:
True or Fiction?

By REPTILIA • February 19, 2026 • 9 min read

Millions of people have heard of David Icke's reptilian theory. World elites are allegedly reptilian beings concealed beneath a human appearance. Where does this idea come from? Why does it fascinate so many? And where does fiction end?

— ◆ —
← Back to Blog

Who is David Icke?

David Icke is a British author and speaker born in 1952, a former professional footballer and TV presenter who reinvented himself as a conspiracy theorist from the 1990s onwards. He is today one of the most controversial figures in the global conspiracy sphere.

His central thesis, developed across dozens of books and lectures worldwide: an elite of extraterrestrial reptilian creatures — the "Archons" — has secretly controlled governments, media and global financial institutions for millennia, concealed beneath human appearances.

Icke's theory has been picked up, amplified and transformed by millions of people on the internet. It has become a global cultural phenomenon, well beyond simple conspiracy belief.

The Timeline of the Reptilian Theory

1991

Icke's Awakening

David Icke announces on a British television programme that he is "the son of God" and that the world is about to change. He is ridiculed. This marks the beginning of his conspiracy reinvention.

1998

The Reptilian Theory is Born

In his book "The Biggest Secret", Icke develops his reptilian theory for the first time. Royal families, US presidents, major financial dynasties are allegedly reptilian hybrids.

2000s

The Internet Explosion

The theory spreads massively across forums, blogs and early video platforms. Millions of people discover and share Icke's ideas. YouTube becomes his primary vehicle.

2010s

Global Cultural Phenomenon

The reptilian theory breaks out of conspiracy circles and enters pop culture, memes and TV series. It inspires artists, creators and brands like REPTILIA.

Today

An Ambiguous Legacy

Icke remains a controversial figure, associated with problematic content condemned by many organisations. His reptilian theory, however, has long since outgrown its creator.

Why It Still Fascinates

The real question is not "is it true?" — it is: why do millions of intelligent people find this idea irresistible?

The answer is psychological and cultural. We live in a complex, opaque world where forces we don't understand seem to decide our lives. Economic crises, wars, pandemics — it all seems orchestrated by invisible entities. The reptilian theory offers a simple, narrative explanation for this complexity.

This is precisely why REPTILIA exists. Not to assert that reptilians control the world — but to explore artistically this universal fascination with the idea of a hidden truth, a concealed power, a reality deeper than what we are shown.

The Difference Between Fiction and Belief

This is the line REPTILIA draws clearly. There is a fundamental difference between:

Artistic fiction — exploring reptilian symbols, playing with archetypes of infiltration and hidden power, creating an immersive universe that questions reality. This is what Matrix, V, Game of Thrones do — and what REPTILIA does.

Literal belief — asserting that real reptilian beings actually control the world. This belief, beyond being factually unfounded, can lead to dangerous outcomes when it targets real human groups.

Powerful fiction asks the right questions without imposing answers. It invites questioning, not accusation. That is the difference between art and propaganda.
