THE ORIGIN OF THE MYTH

In every great civilization in human history, one pattern repeats itself: beings that are half-human, half-reptilian. Gods, guardians, or visitors from the stars.

Egypt calls them the serpent guardians. The Mayans name them Quetzalcoatl, the feathered serpent. The Sumerians speak of the Anunnaki. India venerates the Nagas.

REPTILIA does not claim to reveal the truth. REPTILIA explores these fascinating myths through the lens of streetwear and art.

MYTHOLOGICAL TIMELINE

4000 BC - SUMER
The Anunnaki
Sumerian texts speak of the Anunnaki, "those who came down from the sky." Beings said to have brought knowledge to humanity. Some representations show them with reptilian characteristics.
3000 BC - EGYPT
Wadjet, the Serpent Goddess
The Uraeus, the cobra rearing up on the pharaoh's crown, symbolizes Wadjet, the protective goddess. The serpent as a symbol of divine power and protection.
2000 BC - INDIA
The Nagas
Vedic texts describe the Nagas, half-human half-serpent beings, guardians of underground treasures and secret knowledge. Both venerated and feared.
900 AD - MAYAN CIVILIZATION
Quetzalcoatl
The feathered serpent, a major Mesoamerican deity. Bringer of knowledge, civilization, and the calendar. A promise of return.
Present - DOGON TRIBE, MALI
The Nommos
The Dogon tribe speaks of amphibious visitors from Sirius. How did they know about the existence of Sirius B, invisible to the naked eye, only discovered by our astronomers in 1862?
2026 - TODAY
